### Abstract
We present the Damped <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mrow><mml:mi>Ly</mml:mi><mml:mi>α</mml:mi></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> Toolkit for automated detection and characterization of damped <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mrow><mml:mi>Ly</mml:mi><mml:mi>α</mml:mi></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> absorbers (DLAs) in quasar spectra. Our method uses quasar spectral templates with and without absorption from intervening DLAs to reconstruct observed quasar forest regions. The best-fitting model determines whether a DLA is present while estimating the redshift and HI column density. With an optimized quality cut on detection significance (<inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mi>∆</mml:mi><mml:msubsup><mml:mi>χ</mml:mi><mml:mi>r</mml:mi><mml:mn>2</mml:mn></mml:msubsup><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>0.03</mml:mn></mml:math></inline-formula>), the technique achieves an estimated 80% purity and 79% completeness when evaluated on simulated spectra with <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mi>S</mml:mi><mml:mo>/</mml:mo><mml:mrow><mml:mi>N</mml:mi><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>2</mml:mn></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> that are free of broad absorption lines (BALs). We provide a catalog containing candidate DLAs from the DLA Toolkit detected in DESI DR1 quasar spectra, of which 21 719 were found in <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mi>S</mml:mi><mml:mo>/</mml:mo><mml:mrow><mml:mi>N</mml:mi><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>2</mml:mn></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> spectra with predicted <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mrow><mml:msub><mml:mrow><mml:mi>log</mml:mi></mml:mrow><mml:mn>10</mml:mn></mml:msub><mml:mo>(</mml:mo><mml:msub><mml:mrow><mml:mi>N</mml:mi></mml:mrow><mml:mrow><mml:mi>HI</mml:mi></mml:mrow></mml:msub><mml:mo>)</mml:mo><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>20.3</mml:mn></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> and detection significance <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mi>∆</mml:mi><mml:msubsup><mml:mi>χ</mml:mi><mml:mi>r</mml:mi><mml:mn>2</mml:mn></mml:msubsup><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>0.03</mml:mn></mml:math></inline-formula>. We compare the Damped <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mrow><mml:mi>Ly</mml:mi><mml:mi>α</mml:mi></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> Toolkit to two alternative DLA finders based on a convolutional neural network and Gaussian process models. We present a strategy for combining these three techniques to produce a high-fidelity DLA catalog from DESI DR2 for the <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mrow><mml:mi>Ly</mml:mi><mml:mi>α</mml:mi></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> forest baryon acoustic oscillation measurement. The combined catalog contains 41 152 candidate DLAs with <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mrow><mml:msub><mml:mrow><mml:mi>log</mml:mi></mml:mrow><mml:mn>10</mml:mn></mml:msub><mml:mo>(</mml:mo><mml:msub><mml:mrow><mml:mi>N</mml:mi></mml:mrow><mml:mrow><mml:mi>HI</mml:mi></mml:mrow></mml:msub><mml:mo>)</mml:mo><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>20.3</mml:mn></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula> from quasar spectra with <inline-formula><mml:math><mml:mi>S</mml:mi><mml:mo>/</mml:mo><mml:mrow><mml:mi>N</mml:mi><mml:mo>&amp;gt;</mml:mo><mml:mn>2</mml:mn></mml:mrow></mml:math></inline-formula>. We estimate this sample to be approximately 85% pure and 79% complete when BAL quasars are excluded.
