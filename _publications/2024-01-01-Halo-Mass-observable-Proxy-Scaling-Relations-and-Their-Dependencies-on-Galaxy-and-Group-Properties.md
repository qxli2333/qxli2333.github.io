### Abstract
Based on the DECaLS shear catalog, we study the scaling relations between halo mass (M <SUB>h</SUB>) and various proxies for Sloan Digital Sky Survey central galaxies, including stellar mass (M <SUB>*</SUB>), stellar velocity dispersion (σ <SUB>*</SUB>), abundance-matching halo mass (M <SUB>AM</SUB>), and satellite velocity dispersion (σ <SUB>s</SUB>), and their dependencies on galaxy and group properties. In general, these proxies all have strong positive correlations with M <SUB>h</SUB>, consistent with previous studies. We find that the M <SUB>h</SUB>-M <SUB>*</SUB> and M <SUB>h</SUB>-σ <SUB>*</SUB> relations depend strongly on group richness (N <SUB>sat</SUB>), while the M <SUB>h</SUB>-M <SUB>AM</SUB> and M <SUB>h</SUB>-σ <SUB>s</SUB> relations are independent of it. Moreover, the dependence on the star formation rate (SFR) is rather weak in the M <SUB>h</SUB>-σ <SUB>*</SUB> and M <SUB>h</SUB>-σ <SUB>s</SUB> relations, but very prominent in the other two. σ <SUB>s</SUB> is thus the best proxy among them, and its scaling relation is in good agreement with hydrodynamical simulations. However, estimating σ <SUB>s</SUB> accurately for individual groups/clusters is challenging because of interlopers and the requirement for sufficient satellites. We construct new proxies by combining M <SUB>*</SUB>, σ <SUB>*</SUB>, and M <SUB>AM</SUB>, and find that the proxy with 30% contribution from M <SUB>AM</SUB> and 70% from σ <SUB>*</SUB> can minimize the dependence on N <SUB>sat</SUB> and the SFR. We obtain the M <SUB>h</SUB>-supermassive black hole (SMBH) mass relation via the SMBH scaling relation and find indications for rapid and linear growth phases for the SMBH. We also find that correlations among M <SUB>h</SUB>, M <SUB>*</SUB>, and σ <SUB>*</SUB> change with M <SUB>*</SUB>, indicating that different processes drive the growth of galaxies and SMBHs at different stages.
